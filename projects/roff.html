ROFF

Adhesives and Tile Grouting expert from Pidilite

ROFF was one of the projects that I worked during my time as a User Experience Intern at Interaction One Solutions Pvt. Ltd.

They wanted a redesign of their current website to reflect the needs and connect with their target audience, that is the Consumers, Influencers, and Contractors.

For: Interaction One Solutions Pvt. Ltd.

Duration: May, 2020 to July, 2020

Status: Ongoing

Link: ROFF.in

OVERVIEW

Roff focuses on delivering a comprehensive range of tile and stone Fixing and Care products. They also offers a wide array of products in the categories of Building Repair, Grouting Solutions, and Waterproofing and Sanitary Solutions.

In 2004, Pidilite acquired Roff, establishing it as one of the leading tile adhesive manufacturers in India.

I was assigned this project on the first day of my internship. My role was to do research, ideate on the designs, identify the needs and behaviour of the target audience and create wireframes.

By the end of my two months internship, I had completed the UX wireframes for most of the website, taking into consideration the vast amount of research that I did.

DESIGN PROCESS

This being my first time ever working with a real client, I had a mentor at InteractionOne who enlightened me on the design process that they follow over there.

1. Brief

We sent a creative brief for them to fill out, which would help us find their exact needs and expectations from the project.

The following are our findings -

Target Audience

Male or couple, of age group 35+, who are the following -

  • Consumers
  • Influencers - Word of mouth incentives
  • Contractors - Employs Tile Masons/Fixers
  • Dealers
Information to be conveyed
  • Why use Roff over cement.
  • That Roff is an expert in stone and tile fixing.
Overall Requirements
  • A world class, simple to navigate, elegant website that caters to the wide spectrum of people.
  • A website that is responsive and works on a variety of devices/screen sizes.
  • All products should be approached through a common problem faced by the customers.

2. Research

After finding out the requirements of the client, we moved to research, where we check the usability of the website to find the flaws. This will help us to present a solution that accommodates for better user experience for the website.

We also took a look at the leading competitors' websites to understand how and why they are different. This will also help us identify the good practices from them and mark the areas of improvement.

Usability Matrix

To evaluate the usability of the current website, it is measured relative to users' performance on a given set of test tasks. We had the following results -

Heuristic Evaluation

This is a usability engineering method for finding the usability problems in a user interface design so that they can be attended to as part of an iterative design process.

The following is the summary of what we found -

  • The website has no hierarchy. This makes the interface difficult to navigate.
  • The Navigation Bar is cluttered with options adding to cognitive load for the user.
  • There’s a significant delay for the user to get to the product page from the homepage.
  • The UI looks plain and doesn’t use ROFF’s graphic elements.

3. Analysis

In this phase of the process, we evaluated the competitors' website to identify the functions and features implemented by them, to better understand the competition.

Competitor Analysis

Competitive usability evaluations are a method to determine how a website performs in relation to their competitors’ websites. The comparison can be holistic, ranking sites by some overall site-usability metrics, or it can be more focused, comparing features, content, or design elements across sites.

The goals of this competitive analysis were to identify the content, functionality & feature sets in competing websites, both to inform the design aspects as well as to better understand areas of competitive advantage.

The following is the summary of what we found -

Saint-Gobain Weber
  • The website looks clean and aesthetic.
  • Usage of keywords to identify the products is a good way to highlight its features and sell it to the user.
  • Inclusion of disadvantages tells the user that they care about all the aspects of the product and helps them make an informed decision.
  • Weber’s UI tries to emotionally connect with the user by using people in most of the pictures.
MYK Laticrete
  • The Information Architecture makes it very easy for users to navigate the website.
  • The catalogue of products are divided into sections and cater to specific problems. This helps with the cognitive load for the user.
  • Each product is reached via a specific problem and solution. Hence the user has a clear idea of the product and it’s usage.
  • The UI clearly follows their brand guidelines, with proper use of colours.

In layman's terms, Information architecture (IA) focuses on organizing, structuring, and labeling content in an effective way to help users find information and complete tasks.

Over 40 wireframes were and made reiterated on based on the feedback with the client.

Some of the important screen wireframes are shown below.

Homepage

Navigation Bar consists of 4 options to be clear and concise for the end-user. The 'Find a Dealer' CTA is in bold to draw attention. The menu button opens up to show more navigational links.

The primary target audience is the home-owners. We have categorised Roff’s products such that they are approached through various common problems. Most options are laid out in the homepage itself to reduce time taken to reach the product page.

The top video is placed to grab the attention of the homepage, since this is the primary message to be conveyed. ‘Learn More’ will take the user to a page dedicated for it.

The preview for the Tile Grout Matcher gives the user a sneak peak of the colour selector. It showcases the variety of grout solutions Roff has to provide.

Blogs and Current Trends show the latest trends in tiles, categorised by Living Spaces. Designed such that influencers can show the latest trends to consumers with ease. The blog showcases posts from curators at Roff.

Testimonials show the completed projects using Roff products, along with what the customer says and their photo.

The footer contains the sitemap of the website along with Social Links for quick access.

Discover Solutions

The five main solutions for consumers are tabbed for the ease of switching between the problems.

All product recommendations are on up-front so the user doesn't have to scroll down to find it.

How a problem occurs as well as the application video is shown to clearly tell the consumer what they are dealing with.

Explore Products

This page is intended for professionals who knows what they are looking for.

The Filter By option of products is based on the user journey provided to us. It easily helps the user break down the product selection to what they need.

Adequate description is provided to better understad the product before viewing the page.

Product Page

The features and application areas of the product is shown upfront. The tabs describes the use-cases, benefits, and tech specifications of the product.

The Coverage Calculator provides an idea of how much quantity a user should buy.
