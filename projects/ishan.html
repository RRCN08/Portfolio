OVERVIEW

The main objective of the project was to redesign a soundscape interface, with the Assamese language as a use-case, while creating a compelling visual interface.

A research project under the HSS Dept of IIT-G studied the effects of socio-linguistic factors on vowel pattern variation across different dialects of Assamese spoken in the Brahmaputra valley.

I was tasked to create a well-designed interface which showcases the data collected from this research for anyone in the world to access and interpret it.

PRELIMINARY RESEARCH

I went through multiple research papers detailing data representation and how I can make soundscapes to help achieve it.

The following were my findings -

  • Data is inherently difficult for the human brain to understand and retain. Most adults can only store between 5 and 9 items in their short-term memory.
  • By turning complex numbers and other pieces of information into visuals, data becomes easier to follow and use.
  • Soundscapes are a sonic landscape. It is the total acoustic values related to a particular place.

I also looked at multiple examples of soundscapes such as the Soundsslike Project and the Pop vs Soda page to get a sense of how I can build an interface with sounds as an essential feature.

Information can easily be interpreted when we create an abstraction with the sounds from the soundscape. While the user listens to the sounds, he can read and understand the data served to him via the soundscape interface.

From the research paper, the following data was representable on an interface -

Speech recordings

Speakers' level of pride

Lexical term variations

Age

Gender

Educational background

This design proposes to interpret this vast array of information from the paper onto a medium which is recognisable and easily accessible by everyone.

DESIGN PROCESS

1. Scrutinise

The existing designs for soundscapes were varied.

Soundsslike Project used small dots as identifiers for sounds. On click, it plays the audio along with details about the place.

These were the flaws in their design -

  • The identifiers are too tiny to click.
  • The icons are placed too close together, and misclicks are common.
  • There is a lot of wasted space around the interface which took most of the screen.

The Soundscape World had a different approach. It consists of a player with multiple buttons and a lot more control of the audio. These were categorised into the nature of the sound.

This design was completely different from Soundsslike, and it had the following faults -

  • There is no information regarding the sounds.
  • It doesn't correctly guide the user to its functions.

2. Analyse

These flaws are what makes these interfaces harder to use and navigate. For each of the listed issues, I thought of various ways to tackle them. After a day‚Äôs brainstorming, I came to the following conclusion for my design.

  • It has to be easy to navigate.
  • It should speak for itself.
  • It should utilise its negative space.

3. Ideate

After scourging the project report for hours, we finally concluded that the following data should be represented -

  • Varieties map
  • Level of pride data
  • Audio recordings
  • Graduated distribution of 12 terms

We did multiple iterations of the design throughout the brainstorming period. It was established early on that the interface would encompass a tabbed model for different functions for the page.

Early concepts included dot-like interface similar to the Soundsslike design. It was removed in further iterations as attempts to prevent misclicks were futile. The Pop vs Soda page was used as a reference to make a map which would show details while hovered over. Other iterations included Audio buttons which would take to the location in the map, but this was not preferred as the visual representation was weak.

4. Prototype

We followed a tabbed design to differentiate the functions easily. We used different colours for different screens to indicate the data it‚Äôs providing.

The design consists of 3 screens - Dialects, Listen, and Words.

In the Listen tab, the user can listen to speech recording from various districts of Assam. These, when clicked, show a pop-up with multiple play buttons placed accurately.

The Dialects tab shows the different dialects across the state. One can hover over a region to see what variety they speak, their popularity and how proud they are of their language.

The Words tab shows the distribution of lexical terms. Twelve terms were selected, which had multiple synonyms to study the usage of these words in different regions. We put these terms in a grid, and we showed the distribution using a gradient. The darker the shade, the more frequent the word was used in that region.

5. Review

Torch Red was used as the primary colour for the Listen tab to invoke stimulation since the page‚Äôs function is auditory.

Azure Blue was encompassed in dialects to show the abundance of varieties.

Jade Green was used to showcase the words to represent information.

We developed the platform using HTML5, with Bootstrap 4 as the front-end framework for CSS. JavaScript, accompanied by the jQuery library, was used to handle the back-end of the website. We are currently hosting it on GitHub pages.

FUTURE SCOPE

We completed our first phase of the project and we have even bigger plans for the future, including -

  • Subtitles for the speech recordings.
  • Allowing users to contribute to the project by uploading their recordings.
  • Representing more linguistic data of Assam, making it more informative to the world.
  • A function to record your voice and identify which area your voice matches the most.
