THE CHALLENGE

what

As a second year student at IIT Guwahati, we had our first semester thesis project which lasted for 4 months. I worked under Dr. Abhishek Shrivastava, to create a representation of the various dialects, demographics, and recordings of the Assamese language in a suitable medium.

The project was based off an ongoing research project at the time, under Dr. Priyankoo Sarmah, which studied the effects of socio-linguistic factors on vowel pattern variation across different dialects of Assamese.

SECONDARY RESEARCH

why

Understanding the space

This was the first time I had heard of soundscapes, and it was an interesting topic. I spent the initial weeks trying to gather knowledge about soundscapes and how they work.

Along with this, I also spent time on the basics of data visualisation, because at the core of it, soundscape is a auditory depiction of data.

Definition of soundscapes

A term first coined by the Canadian composer R. Murray Schafer, a soundscape is a sonic landscape. It is the total acoustic values related to a certain place.

Information through sounds

Soundscapes can be understood only through peoplesâ€™ perceptions. Information can easily be interpreted when we create an abstraction with the sounds from the soundscape.

Data visualisation

Can be defined as information which has been abstracted in some schematic form, including attributes or variables for the units of information.

Why visualisation is important

Data is inherently difficult for the human brain to understand and retain. Most adults can store between 5 and 9 items in their short-term memory.

The concept

I decided to divide the design into 3 sections. These had different colours, indicating the data is different from one another. I started off with some concept sketches.

Listen Tab

The user can listen to speech recordings from various districts of Assam. These, when clicked, show a pop-up with multiple play buttons placed accurately.

Dialects Tab

Shows the different dialects across the state. One can hover over a region to see what variety they speak, their popularity and how proud they are of their language.

Words Tab

Shows the distribution of lexical terms. Twelve terms were selected, which had multiple synonyms to study the usage of these words in different regions. We put these terms in a grid, and we showed the distribution using a gradient. The darker the shade, the more frequent the word was used in that region.
